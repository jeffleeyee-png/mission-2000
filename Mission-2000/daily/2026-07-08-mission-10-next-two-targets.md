# Mission 10 - Next Two Targets

## 1. Temple

**Company:** Temple

**Country:** India

**Product:** Wearable health device positioned around the temple/head area. Public reporting describes it as measuring cerebral blood flow / brain-health or high-precision performance signals. Exact sensor stack is not public.

**Funding:** $54M first funding round, reported valuation around $190M. Sources: https://m.economictimes.com/tech/funding/deepinder-goyals-wearable-startup-temple-raises-54-million-from-steadview-peak-xv-others/articleshow/128852025.cms and https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/business/india-business/deepinder-goyals-health-tech-startup-temple-secures-54-million-funding-valued-at-190-million/articleshow/128860122.cms

**Traction:** Recent activity is strong: waitlist announced in February 2026, hiring drive announced in February 2026, and first batch of 100 devices reportedly manufactured for select users in May 2026. Source: https://m.economictimes.com/tech/startups/eternal-founder-deepinder-goyal-drops-100-temple-wearables-but-theres-a-catch/articleshow/130830606.cms

**Why Jeff can help:** Temple is at exactly the awkward hardware stage: funded, visible, prototype / first-batch hardware exists, but broad consumer release is not yet proven. Jeff can offer a production-readiness checklist for small physiological wearables: sensor repeatability, skin-contact materials, battery, charging, enclosure, waterproofing/sweat resistance, pilot QA, supplier backup, and certification planning.

**Manufacturing risk:** High. Risks include sensor accuracy, placement consistency, skin comfort, battery safety, sweat resistance, charging reliability, calibration, pilot-user returns, and medical/wellness claim boundaries.

**Best public contact route:** Deepinder Goyal public X / Instagram announcement route, plus Temple hiring/waitlist references. A confirmed official Temple contact page was not found. Best opening should reference the May 2026 first-batch report and February 2026 hiring/waitlist news, not a generic supplier pitch.

**Jeff Score:** 91/100

## 2. Omi / Based Hardware

**Company:** Omi / Based Hardware Inc.

**Country:** United States

**Product:** AI wearable and app ecosystem for recording conversations, summarizing meetings, creating tasks/reminders, and running open-source integrations. Omi also lists Omi Glass and accessories on its website.

**Funding:** Funding not confirmed in public search. Strong traction exists instead: Omi's website says it is trusted by 300,000+ professionals; The Verge reported 5,000 early devices; Investors.com reported CES 2026 activity and Omi's speaker-recognition, app marketplace, and smart-prompt features. Sources: https://www.omi.me/, https://www.theverge.com/2025/1/8/24338750/omi-ai-wearable-friend-companion, and https://www.investors.com/news/technology/ces-2026-wearable-ai-gadgets-could-be-next-big-thing/

**Traction:** 300,000+ professionals claimed on the official website; 5,000 early devices reported by The Verge; recent CES 2026 coverage; official website currently shows products, accessories, GitHub, Discord, company email, and reseller/affiliate routes.

**Why Jeff can help:** Omi has real hardware but likely needs low-cost, repeatable consumer electronics execution: microphone quality, battery, charging, casing, clip/lanyard/wristband accessories, packaging, QA, and supplier redundancy. The company is also adding form factors like Omi Glass, which increases supply-chain complexity.

**Manufacturing risk:** Medium-high. Risks include microphone consistency, battery life, LED/privacy indicator, enclosure durability, accessory quality, charger reliability, firmware flashing, final test fixtures, and packaging for multiple wearing modes.

**Best public contact route:** Official website / public email: https://www.omi.me/ and `help@omi.me`. Backup routes: official GitHub at https://github.com/BasedHardware/omi and company social links listed on the Omi website.

**Jeff Score:** 85/100
