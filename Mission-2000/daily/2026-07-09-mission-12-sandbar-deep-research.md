# Mission 12 - Sandbar Deep Research

## Executive Summary

Sandbar is a New York City interface startup building Stream Ring, a first-generation AI smart ring for private voice capture, notes, haptics, music controls, and an iOS companion app. It is a strong Jeff target because it has real preorder demand, small-company signals, a complex miniaturized hardware product, and no public evidence of a mature manufacturing organization or major contract manufacturer.

Best angle: send a useful "Batch 4 readiness checklist" for smart rings, focused on size-SKU yield, battery, mic acoustic consistency, touchpad reliability, water resistance, charger fit, final QA, and supplier backup.

## Manufacturing Risks

### Confirmed

- **Ring hardware:** Stream Ring is a smart ring worn on the index finger.
- **Charging design:** Charger included; charges via USB-C. The Verge describes a flat disc with a U-shaped holder.
- **Battery:** All-day rechargeable battery. Sandbar also describes a custom curved battery.
- **BLE:** Product page says Stream Ring connects via Bluetooth.
- **Microphone:** Touch-activated private mic, not always listening.
- **Touch sensor:** Glass touchpad; hold, tap, double tap, and swipe controls.
- **Haptics:** LRA for silent haptic feedback.
- **Materials:** Aluminum outer surface, resin inner surface, glass touchpad.
- **Water resistance:** Water resistant to rain and handwashing; WIRED says waterproof at launch, but the official product page uses water-resistant language.
- **Sizing:** Sizes 5-13, worn on index finger, with sizing kit.

### Likely

- **Size SKU complexity:** Sizes 5-13 create inventory, tooling, fit, and yield challenges.
- **Mic acoustic consistency:** Side openings, sealing, resin/aluminum structure, and hand position can affect capture quality.
- **Touch reliability:** Glass touchpad plus sweat, oil, rain, and handwashing may create false inputs or missed inputs.
- **Battery risk:** Custom curved battery adds supplier, swelling, cycle-life, charging, and UN38.3 documentation risk.
- **Water resistance risk:** Mic openings, touch surface, charger interface, and resin/aluminum bonding are likely high-risk areas.
- **Charger fit risk:** A U-shaped holder must fit multiple ring sizes and maintain contact over repeated use.
- **Final QA risk:** Product needs per-unit test for mic, BLE, IMU, haptics, touch, battery, charging, and cosmetic finish.

## Jeff Opportunity

**Why Jeff should contact Sandbar**

Sandbar has a real product, real preorder demand, and a difficult small-form-factor hardware ramp. Batch 1-3 are sold out, Batch 4 is still pending, and the product has many components that can fail in production: mic, touchpad, haptics, BLE, curved battery, charger, water resistance, and size-SKU fit.

**Exact problem Jeff is most qualified to solve**

Jeff should help Sandbar reduce Batch 4 manufacturing risk by offering a concise smart-ring production checklist: supplier backup, fixture-based final test, charger fit validation across sizes, water-resistance test plan, battery documentation, packaging/returns readiness, and Shenzhen/Dongguan supplier options for accessories, charger, QA fixtures, and assembly support.

**What Jeff should NEVER mention**

- Do not open with "I can find you a factory."
- Do not mention Foxconn, Flex, Huaqin, FIH, or big contract manufacturing.
- Do not claim to know their BOM, factory, suppliers, or defects.
- Do not pitch generic sourcing services.
- Do not criticize their current manufacturing.
- Do not lead with price, commission, or fees.
- Do not focus on AI software; the value is hardware execution.
