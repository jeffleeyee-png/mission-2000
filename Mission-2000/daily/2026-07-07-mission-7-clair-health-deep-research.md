## Manufacturing Risk Checklist

**Context:** Clair appears to be moving from a sold-out 5,000-unit first production run toward launch and a larger waitlist. The items below are manufacturing-risk analysis, not confirmed Clair problems.

- **Battery:** Confirm cycle life, swelling risk, capacity variance, shipping compliance, thermal behavior, and supplier backup before larger runs.
- **BLE:** Test pairing reliability across iOS/Android versions, antenna performance on different wrist sizes, firmware update recovery, and field logs.
- **Sensors:** Since sensor type is not public, verify calibration drift, skin-contact consistency, sweat/skin-tone/body-temperature variation, and per-unit test limits.
- **Waterproofing:** Validate sweat, shower, handwashing, gasket aging, charging-port corrosion, and post-drop water ingress.
- **Enclosure:** Watch tolerance stack-up around sensor window, charging interface, antenna zone, cosmetic finish, and adhesive or ultrasonic weld consistency.
- **Strap/materials:** Test skin irritation, discoloration, sweat resistance, clasp fatigue, size coverage, and replacement process.
- **Charging:** Validate pogo-pin or dock alignment if used, magnet strength if used, cable quality, charger safety, and user-error cases.
- **Packaging:** Prepare retail-ready inserts, regulatory labels, serial tracking, return labels, and first-use instructions that reduce support burden.
- **QC:** Add incoming inspection, golden-sample checks, BLE pairing test, sensor test, waterproof sample test, charge/discharge sample test, cosmetic AQL, and serialized final inspection.
- **Returns:** Define early failure categories: battery, strap, charging, app pairing, skin comfort, bad readings, cosmetic defects, and buyer remorse.
- **Certification:** Confirm FCC, Bluetooth SIG, battery transport, RoHS/REACH, California Prop 65, and medical/wellness claim review. No public FCC/CE filing was found.
- **Supplier backup:** Identify second sources for battery, strap, enclosure, charging cable/dock, packaging, and final assembly before demand spikes.
